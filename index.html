The Scalability Solution: ZK-Rollups

This section explores the fundamental challenge of blockchain scalability and how Zero-Knowledge Rollups (ZK-Rollups) provide a powerful solution. You will learn about the core concepts and compare the two main types of ZK proofs.

The Blockchain Trilemma

Blockchains face a constant trade-off between three crucial properties: **Security**, **Decentralization**, and **Scalability**. It's incredibly difficult to maximize all three at once. Ethereum prioritizes security and decentralization, which creates a bottleneck for scalability, leading to high fees and slow transactions.

Layer 2 solutions like ZK-Rollups are designed to solve this by handling transactions off-chain, inheriting Ethereum's security while dramatically boosting scalability. This is not a theoretical concept; it's a practical constraint that L2s are designed to mitigate by using the L1's security as a foundation.

Fundamentals of Zero-Knowledge Rollups

ZK-Rollups, also known as Validity-Rollups, achieve scalability by bundling transactions off-chain and generating a cryptographic proof of their validity. This succinct proof is then submitted to the Ethereum mainnet, providing immediate finality.

ZK-SNARKs vs. ZK-STARKs

ZK-Rollups rely on cryptographic proofs. The two dominant types are SNARKs and STARKs. While both serve to verify computations, they have key differences in their design and trade-offs. Starknet is built on the STARK proof system.

Building with Cairo

Cairo is the native, Rust-inspired language for writing provable programs on Starknet. This section introduces Cairo's core concepts, the development workflow, and essential security practices. Explore the tools that make up the robust developer ecosystem.

What is Cairo?

Cairo is a Turing-complete language specifically designed to generate STARK proofs. This means any program written in Cairo can produce a cryptographic proof of its own correct execution. Its potential extends beyond blockchain to any field requiring verifiable computation, like AI and gaming.

Key Programming Concepts

  • `felt252` Type: The primary data type is a field element, a 252-bit number. Arithmetic is modular, requiring careful handling to avoid overflows.
  • Native Account Abstraction: All accounts are smart contracts, enabling flexible features like social recovery, session keys, and paying gas in any token.
  • State Management: Contract storage is managed explicitly, providing clear control over data persistence.
  • Functions: Functions can be `External` (callable by users) or `Internal` (only callable within the contract).

Development Workflow and Essential Tooling

The development workflow for Starknet dApps involves a suite of specialized tools. After writing your contracts in Cairo, you use **Scarb** for compilation and **Starkli** for deployment. For frontend integration, **Starknet.js** is the primary library, and for testing, **Starknet Devnet** is invaluable for local testing. It's crucial to follow security best practices and consider third-party audits.

1. Staking (Economic Security)

A Proof-of-Stake (PoS) mechanism where validators stake STRK tokens to secure the network and prevent attacks. Phase 1 is already live.

2. Operation (SN Stack)

The open-sourcing of the Starknet Stack (SN Stack) allows anyone to run a node, fostering a decentralized network of operators.

A phased approach to hand over control of protocol upgrades and treasury management to the community and STRK token holders.
